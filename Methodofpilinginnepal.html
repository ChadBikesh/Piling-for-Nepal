Methods Exist in Nepal

Continuous Flight Auger (CFA)

The Continuous Flight Auger (CFA) process is virtually vibration free and one of the quietest forms of piling, making it ideal for environmentally sensitive areas.The method enables piles to be formed in water-bearing strata, without the need for casing or bentonite. It is suitable for constructing piles in most strata: gravels, sands, silts, clays and soft rocks and in mixtures of strata.

Drilling With Kelly System

This drilling method includes kelly bar which acts as drill rod. Botttom of kelly bar is connected with drilling tools. The drilling tools could be core barrel, auger and bucket depending upon the nature of soil strata. The diamter of drillig hole ranges from Ø600 mm to Ø2000 mm. For this drilling method, big or heavy piling machines are used. Basic Principle of Operation:
The piling operation begins with the drilling operation on the soil up to the predetermined depth of hole. For this purpose, casing (circular steel tube ) is fitted on the rotatory drive of machine. Casing is pressed downward under the soil level with the help of rotatory force. The length of casing varies with the purpose of works and application. Normally the length of casing is 1.5 meter, 2 meter and so on.. After casing is inserted below the soil level (note: one end of casing lies below the soil level and one end of casing lies on the top level of soil level), now bucket or other drill tools performs its action.Bucket or other drill tool helps to remove the soil particle that is trapped inside the casing, is dragged outside of casing and make the bucket empty from soil particles. This process continued until all the soil particles is removed up to the depth of inserted casing thus creating bored hole up to the depth of that casing. Again the casing is added, and drilling operation continues until final required depth is achieved.

Full Cased Micropiling

Full cased bored pile techniques requires both casing and drilling tools up to the required bored depth. It is mostly used in the micro piling. Normally the diameter of micropiling ranges upto 300 mm. The drilling tools for this method are drill rods, drill bit, casing and casing shoe/crown. This full cased drilling method is used for loose soil strata: gravel mix with sand, small boulders big boulders etc.
FLexibility of Micropiling Machine:
Micropiling machines are medium sized and portable. They can be transported to rural area's highways easily. Mostly they are most suitable for himalayas region for Hydropower Station. Micropiling are used for slope protection, piling foundation for penstock and bridges in Hydropower Project. They are economical and have capability to drill on hard rocks. They are equipped with DTH Hammer and delivers excellent drilling performance saving both time and money.
Basic Principle of Operation:
In this techniques, both drilling tool and casing is connected and rotates with the rotatory drive of machine. One end of the drill rod usually of 1 meter is connected with the drill bit (the actual drilling tools) and other end is free. The free end of drill rod is later attached with another drill rod in order to get the required length for boring a hole or help drill bit to drill upto the required depth below soil level. Same condition apply in the casing as well, but in case of drill bit, casing crown is used at one end of the casing. Hence, if depth of 12 meter hole is required to be bored, then this techniques required both 12 meter of casing and drill rod. The compressed air is feed inside the drill rod to the drill bit, in order to push out the soil particles that is trapped inside of casing, during the time of drilling. Both casing and drill rod receive rotatory downward force with percussion to make the drilling operation effective and efficient. The extension of drill rods and casing continues until final required depth of bored holes is achieved.

Direct Mud Circulation (DMC)

Direct Mud Circulation method of pile foundation installation is used for bored cast in-situ piles. Direct Mud Circulation method is best suited when working-space is a huge constraint. In Industries and power plants where there is a labyrinth of pipelines snaking all around, DMC comes to the rescue. While the other methods are comparatively expensive, DMC is a fairly cheaper method to install piles. The apparatus basically comprises a Tripod with pulley, winch machine, wire-rope, Chisel, drill pipes, tremie pipes, a vertical pump and a pit where bentonite powder is mixed with fresh water. Fig d A tripod as the name suggests is a three-legged structure which is mounted with a pulley at the apex to maneuver the chisel that bores the earth, which is further appended to drill pipes in tandem with a wire rope that is ultimately knotted to the winch machine. To make it sound simple, the chisel is the primary boring tool and the drill pipes are mere appendages that provide the depth, and also, suffuse the bore with bentonite suspension. The number of drill pipes required varies depending on the depth of the bore required. Besides this, a vertical pump is installed in the bentonite pit so as to pump the bentonite solution into the bore hole through drill pipes and chisel. Basic Basic Principle of Operation:
The method is termed as Direct Mud Circulation because it involves the circulation of mud from the pile bore directly to the bentonite pit where the pump reuses the bentonite slurry leaving alone the settled mud.
1) The Tripod is made to stand around the target pile (two legs around the pile and one into the slot provided in winch machine) and a plumb bob is then hung down the pulley (mounted on the tripod) aiming the center of the target pile so as to obtain the center for the chisel. The maximum tolerance permissible for piles with diameter 600mm or more is 75mm or D/2, whichever is more.
2) Once the position of winch machine and the tripod is fixed, casing is installed into the earth keeping the center point intact. A wall made up of sacks filled with rubble or soil is created around the pile-area so as to channel an artificial conduit directed towards the bentonite pit. This channel directs the mixture of mud and bentonite slurry coming out from the bore to the bentonite pit.
3) The topmost drill pipe is connected with a hose emerging from the vertical pump (installed in the bentonite) pit sending fresh bentonite slurry to the borehole through a fine opening in chisel and when the bore hole is filled to the brink, it flows back to the bentonite pit along with the mud through the artificial conduit. In this way the mud from the bore keeps on moving up the borehole and is directed to the bentonite tank.
4) It is preferred to use fresh and clean water for the preparation of bentonite suspension as the minutest impurities may alter the properties of a suspension. In case, clean water is not available at the site, special additives such as Ferrosome lignosulphate in combination with soda ash, phosphates and flocculants are added.
5) Chisel is then driven inside the casing and the earth is bored until the chisel has fully gone down inside the earth. Drill pipes are attached to the chisel in tandem and the boring progresses by maneuvering the winch machine until the requisite depth is bored. Flushing is done all the while during the pile boring. After the completion of bore, chisel is brought outside and the second flushing of the bore hole is done to remove all the mud and impurities.

Reinforcement Installation

After boring is completed from one of the drilling method mentioned above,then reinforcement cage is inserted into that hole. The diameter of reinforcement cage depends on the diameter or size of the required pile. If there is two or more reinforcement cages is required to satisfy the length of the pile, then they are welded together with proper lapping distance. The lapping distance is also depends on the diameter of reinforcement rod, normally lapping distance is kept to 45*D, where D is diameter of reinforcement rod.

Concreteing

Finally, piling is completed after concreting is done upto the required depth or length of pile into the bored holes. If casing is used for drilling techniques, then concreting and removing of casing is done simultaneously or as per the condition(depends on level of concreted depth)
